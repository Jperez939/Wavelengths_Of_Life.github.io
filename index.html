WAVELENGTHS
OF LIFE

FEATURING “THE WAVELENGTHS OF LIFE”
WRITTEN BY EMILY WILCOX, PUBLISHED IN ILLUMINATION BY MEDIUM

BY JOSELYN PEREZ

This phrase refers to the concept of accessing one’s highest potential
by the distance between moments of joy and despair in life. This
definition can also be used in terms of the visible spectrum and
health, which is depending on the range of wavelengths of light
(visible spectrum) that one can see, different people will have
different effects from this light to their body and emotion.

STANZA 01

LINES 1-4

Life comes in wavelengths,

Different strips of sun,

Shades of each emotion,

Blinding when overdone.

STANZA 02

LINES 5-8

Amplitudes so deafening,

A starburst of pure sound.

But then life can sidle up and whisper,

“What meaning have you found?”

STANZA 03

LINES 9-12

Colours to capture memories,

The frequencies of us all.

There is no moment not made of light,

The largest and the small.

STANZA 04

LINES 13-16

We each absorb wavelengths,

Or reflect them back to the eye.

Our interference constructive,

As long as we choose to try.

STANZA 05

LINES 17-20

But to see these strips of lightness,

Our lenses must be clean.

So that when we gaze up at the stars,

We’ll know that we too are seen.
